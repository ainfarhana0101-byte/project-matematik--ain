The Bioscience Problem

In Malaysian palm oil estates (such as those managed by MPOB or FELDA), tracking the Fresh Fruit Bunch (FFB) yield per hectare relative to nitrogen fertilizer application is vital. While nitrogen increases growth, over-fertilization leads to toxicity, environmental runoff, and diminished returns.

By finding the derivative $f'(x)$ of our yield curve, agro-technologists can determine the exact point of diminishing returns—where adding more fertilizer actually slows down the rate of production increase, or even causes it to drop.

Data Reference baseline: Adapted from the Malaysian Palm Oil Board (MPOB) Fruit Bunch Yield Optimization Models and agronomic response curves for Tenera cultivars.

The Mathematical Model

Yield Function $f(x)$ (Tonnes/Ha)
$f(x) = -0.0005x^2 + 0.16x + 12$
First Derivative $f'(x)$ (Rate of Change)
$f'(x) = -0.001x + 0.16$

Where $x$ is the Nitrogen input ($\text{kg/ha/year}$) ranging from $0$ to $240\text{ kg}$, and $f(x)$ is the total annual FFB yield ($\text{tonnes/ha}$).
