Layman explanation

The derivative f′(x) tells you how fast the bunch is gaining weight at that week. Higher f′(x) = faster filling; near harvest, f′(x) → 0.

Bioscience Scenario: Oil Palm FFB Development

In Malaysian estates, an oil palm fresh fruit bunch (FFB) grows for ~20–24 weeks after effective pollination before harvest. The rate of growth is not constant: it accelerates in the middle phase (oil accumulation) and slows as the bunch approaches physiological maturity. Understanding f′(x), the instantaneous rate of change, helps schedule harvests, plan labour and logistics, and anticipate oil extraction rates in mills.

Model and Formulas

We model average bunch weight with a logistic curve (x in weeks after pollination, kg as output):
f(x) = K / (1 + e^{-r (x - x0)})

The derivative (growth rate) is:
f′(x) = (K · r · e^{-r (x - x0)}) / (1 + e^{-r (x - x0)})^2

  • K ≈ 25 kg (typical upper bunch weight for tenera palms in good conditions)
  • r ≈ 0.40 week⁻¹ (steepness of growth)
  • x0 ≈ 14 weeks (inflection point where growth is fastest)

These values are realistic approximations used for teaching. Actual estates calibrate parameters by cultivar, age, site, and season.

How to use this demo

  1. Observe the blue curve f(x): predicted FFB weight at each week after pollination.
  2. Observe the purple curve f′(x): predicted growth rate (kg/week). It peaks near the inflection (~week 14) and declines toward harvest.
  3. Click on the chart at any week x. The app computes f(x) and f′(x) using the above formulas and explains what that rate means in plain language.
  4. Use this concept in SFP: Agro Product Technology to justify harvest timing, labour planning, and mill intake scheduling based on peak or declining growth rates.

Key takeaways

  • Derivative = rate: f′(x) quantifies how fast mass (or moisture/oil) is accumulating.
  • Peak f′(x) identifies the rapid filling phase; low f′(x) signals approaching maturity/harvest.
  • Operations: When f′(x) starts dropping consistently, schedule harvest rounds to capture optimal quality without delay losses.
